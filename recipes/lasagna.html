Lasagna

The traditional 'Lasagna' dish is prepared with meat, and even though people have found ways to create wonderful and tasty vegetarian alternatives of Lasagna, today we will focus on the traditional lasagna with beef. Typically known as an Italian dish, lasagna has made its way to everyones top favorite food with its many differnt igredients, wonderful, unique taste and of course we cannot forget the delicious melted cheese on top that everyone desires!

Lasagna can be a rather calorie-loaded dish, so maybe it isn't an every day food, but today we want to put a smile on your faces by giving you a simple lasagna recipes with ingredients anyone can find, so that even the unluckiest cooks (like me) can master this dish!

Ingredients

  • half a pound of ground beef
  • 28 ounces of spagetti sauce
  • 1 can (14.5 ounces) of diced tomatoes
  • half an onion, shopped
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 2 tsp dried basil
  • 2 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 2 cups of dried mafalda noodles
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese

Directions

  1. Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat. Cook and stir beef in the hot skillet until browned and crumbly, 5 to 7 minutes. Drain and discard grease. Add spaghetti sauce, tomatoes, onion, garlic, basil, oregano, salt, and pepper. Cook over low heat until sauce is hot, about 15 minutes.
  2. Meanwhile, fill a large pot with lightly salted water and bring to a rolling boil. Cook mafalda noodles at a boil until tender yet firm to the bite, about 8 minutes. Drain.
  3. Add cooked and drained noodles to the sauce and stir until completely coated. Sprinkle mozzarella cheese on top.
  4. Set an oven rack about 6 inches from the heat source and preheat the oven's broiler.
  5. Place skillet under the hot broil and cook until cheese is golden and bubbly, 3 to 5 minutes.
